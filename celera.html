— On Philosophy

We cook the archipelago the way it should have been written — with restraint, with provenance, with a single point of view.

— Chef-Proprietor

Each menu is a fourteen-course micro-seasonal narrative built around what arrives that week from our growers in Benguet, the divers of Mindoro, and a handful of Visayan producers we have worked with for years.

Nothing on the counter exists to impress. Every fermentation, every cure, every fold of dough is in service of a single sentence the dish is trying to finish.

The menu rotates every twenty-one days. No dish returns the same way twice.

A selection from the current rotation, served in the order the kitchen has written them. Pairings curated by our sommelier — wine, sake, or a non-alcoholic infusion flight.

Course 02 · Cold

Lapu-Lapu Crudo, green mango ash

Line-caught Mindoro grouper, cured forty minutes in tablea-salt, dressed with cold-pressed coconut oil, charred green mango ash, and a single leaf of sea purslane harvested that morning.

Course 07 · Broth

Aged Wagyu Sinigang, kamias five ways

Forty-day dry-aged short rib over a clarified consommé of kamias, tamarind leaf, and fermented green tomato. Finished tableside with a single shaving of smoked beef fat.

Course 09 · Grain

Adlai & Bone-Marrow Bibingka

Heirloom adlai from Bukidnon, roasted in clay, folded into a savory bibingka batter with smoked carabao butter, roasted bone marrow, and aged kesong puti from Laguna.

Course 04 · Sea

Bolinao Uni, palapa & pandan oil

Northern Luzon sea urchin laid over a warm custard of native chicken egg, finished with Maranao palapa, pandan-infused oil, and crisp toasted pinipig.

Course 11 · Main

Heritage Duck, aged adobo glaze

Pateros heritage duck, dry-aged fourteen days, roasted on bamboo coals. Glazed with a three-year adobo reduction, served with charred mustasa and a single confit garlic clove.

Course 13 · Sweet

Davao Cacao, tablea & coconut sap

Single-origin 72% Davao cacao, set with coconut sap caramel, smoked muscovado, and a wafer of toasted pinipig. Served with a small cup of cold kapeng barako.
